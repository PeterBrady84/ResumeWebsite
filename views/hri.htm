Horse Racing Ireland

Camera Operator

February 2001 - December 2005 (4 Years 10 Months)


Read More about Horse Racing Ireland

Horse Racing Ireland is the national authority for thoroughbred horse racing in Ireland. The Camera Patrol division used television cameras and equipment to film races from different angles for exclusive use by the racecourse stewards. These images were to ensure that the races were run in accordance to racing rules and provided visual evidence for stewards enquires and appeals.

Main Responsibilities
• To provide good quality camera work and know the different aspects of the race to watch for depending on the camera position and role on the day.
• Job entailed travelling all over Ireland on a near-daily basis.
• Good problem solving ability and willingness to fix issues that arose between races or with your own equiptment.
• This job demanded extreme level of work during busy periods, in all type of locations and weather conditions.
